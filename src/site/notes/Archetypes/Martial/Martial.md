*You have a strong sense of discipline and focus when it comes to getting things done.*

# Starting Move: Focus
When you spend time investigating a person, place, or thing and [[Moves/Basic Moves/Assess\|Assess]], you can forgo a question and gain a Focus - a particular target that you hone in on and plan to investigate more thoroughly. Write it down. At any time, use a Focus and choose one:
- While you're in pursuit of the target and you make a [[System Mechanics/Move\|combat or archetype move]] that lets you make a choice, choose one more option.
- When you [[System Mechanics/Combat\|exchange damage]] with the target (or in an attempt to get to the target), choose a weapon property and assign it to your weapon for that exchange.
- [[Moves/Basic Moves/Pray\|Pray]] without making a sacrifice and ask about the target.
When you [[Moves/Core Moves/End a Session\|End a Session]], also ask if you have any active Focuses. You can have a number of active Focuses at a time equal to your [[System Mechanics/Attributes/Ether\|Ether]].

# Divine Moves
*Start with all divine moves locked - you don't have access to them. After level 5, when you gain an [[System Mechanics/XP\|advancement]], you can unlock a divine move. When you use it, lock it again.*
- Divine Determination. When you take damage that would kill you or force you to [[Moves/Core Moves/Face Death\|face death]], you may refuse it. Describe how your divine blood surges to keep you standing. Unlock and clear a condition and restore your [[System Mechanics/Health\|health]] until you are no longer [[System Mechanics/Combat\|bloodied]].
- Clue In. Describe an aspect of your investigation in which you've involved your [[Bonds and Depth/Relationships, Depth, and Bonds\|bond-mates]]. For the rest of the [[System Mechanics/Scene\|scene]], when you use a Focus, choose a bond-mate to gain the same benefits.
- Big Brain. Describe a moment of brilliant deduction or analysis - your Eureka moment. Use a Focus to increase your [[System Mechanics/Attributes/Ether\|Ether]] by 1 for the rest of the scene (max. 4).
