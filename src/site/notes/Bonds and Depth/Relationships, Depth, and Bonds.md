As you spend time adventuring with and battling alongside your fellow PCs, it's only natural that the group dynamics will change over time. Relationships reflect this change, including what you know about the other PCs, what you think of them, and what you plan to do with them. The details and depth of relationships between PCs are managed independently. When you adventure with another PC, start with a relationship with each PC that has no details, no current depth, and three maximum depth. If your backstories are tied together you may gain up to one depth and adjust the details of your relationship accordingly. Remember, a deeper relationship doesn't necessarily mean you like each other more.

While relationships represent the readily apparent connections and emotions you have with another character, bonds represent the unbreakable ties between you and them. They aren't always positive, but they help you work together and overcome strife alongside your bond-mate. While relationships and their depths are managed individually, bonds and their moves are mutually shared. When you reach maximum depth with another PC, [[Moves/Basic Moves/Forge a Bond\|forge a bond]].

There is no limit to the number of bonds you can have. If your bond-mate dies, you lose access to all shared bond moves.
