When you find yourself in a tough situation, sometimes the only way out is to start swinging. When you enter combat, you can still make moves and help others, but things are moving at a faster pace and there are higher stakes. The GM continues to describe the situation that one or more PCs find themselves in, ask what they do, and deliver appropriate consequences depending on their choices and rolls.

# Actions and Moves
Combat is turn-based, with each round of combat taking place over ~30 seconds. Each PC can take up to three actions on their turn or on someone else's turn (aside from on their first turn, which is determined when you [[Moves/Combat Moves/Enter Battle\|Enter Battle]]). Actions encompass pretty much everything you can do in the span of a few seconds, from grabbing and using an item to sprinting from point A to point B. Most moves require a single action, such as taking a few seconds to [[Moves/Basic Moves/Assess\|Assess]] an enemy's next move, but certain moves (particularly combat moves like [[Moves/Combat Moves/All Out Attack\|All Out Attack]] or [[Moves/Combat Moves/Take Control\|Take Control]]), may require multiple actions across multiple PCs to execute. Moves that are consequential, like [[Moves/Core Moves/Reach Your Limit\|Reach Your Limit]] or [[Moves/Core Moves/Face Death\|Face Death]], trigger without you having to take any actions.

At the start of each of your turns after the first, your action count resets to 3. Unused actions from your previous turn are lost. If you feel like there isn't a lot that you can do on your turn, keep your actions! You can use them to [[Moves/Basic Moves/Support\|Support]] someone or [[Moves/Basic Moves/Provide Aid\|Provide Aid]] later on, when it might be more effective.

# Damage
Some combat moves can lead to you exchanging damage with an enemy - you simultaneously deal damage to each other and lose that much [[System Mechanics/Health\|health]]. When you deal damage, the amount depends on your equipment and relevant moves. An enemy's damage depends on their numbers, techniques, and ferocity. A simple minion might deal 1 damage, while a group of minions or a solitary monster might deal 3+ damage, and an elder monster or immortal might deal 6+ damage.

Depending on the circumstances, you might not take damage at all. If an enemy is distracted or you secured an edge to protect your allies, you can mitigate damage. If you're using a ranged weapon from afar and the enemy doesn't have a way of hurting you, you can avoid taking damage. Pay attention to the minutia of a battle and get creative with ways to protect yourself! You can also use [[Items/Armour\|defensive equipment]] and certain archetype moves to mitigate damage.

## Bloodied
While your [[System Mechanics/Health\|health]] is at or below half of its maximum, you’re bloodied and you take -3 to all combat moves. While similar to conditions, bloodied is not a condition itself; it cannot be marked or cleared the way other conditions can. As soon as your health returns to above half, you immediately clear bloodied. When it returns to below half, mark bloodied again. 

## Enemy Behaviour
Enemies don't usually act on their own initiative, but they will respond to your moves and actions - especially when you fail on a move. Most of your enemies will be bloodthirsty, savage, terrifying monsters that are incredibly dangerous but also incredibly thick-headed. Older, wiser monsters are likely to display a higher level of intelligence and strategy. Demigods, spirits, and other immortal figures may be even smarter than you.

# Movement and Distance
You can move up to a number of squares (sq.) equal to 10 + [[System Mechanics/Attributes/Attributes\|Silver]] as part of taking any action. You can also use an action to dash, doubling that distance until the start of your next turn.

Using certain moves and abilities, as well as using certain weapons, will require you to be within a certain distance of an enemy or another PC. Distance is measured as follows:
- Melee: Within 1 sq.
- Very Close: Within 3 sq.
- Close: Within 6 sq.
- Far: Within 12 sq.
- Very Far: Everything else.
